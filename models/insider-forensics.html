WHAT THE TWO LENSES ARE — and why they live in one view now
Improbable record (binomial)
The account won far more long-shot bets than its entry odds allow. We compute the exact probability that record is luck (the “1 in N”) — the validated backbone. The list is ranked by a composite suspicion score that blends this improbability with corroboration breadth, pre-event timing, and magnitude, so a broadly-corroborated insider isn’t out-ranked by a small lucky-looking streak.
Out-profited the market (cross-sectional)
Even without a long-shot record, an account can bet a favorite and out-profit every other trader in that market — the favorite-odds insider the long-shot test is blind to. Folded in here, gated hard: the bet won, was outsized, the wallet is net-profitable, and on-chain structure corroborates.
Near-perfect across markets (cross-category)
A near-perfect record across many diverse, moderate-odds markets (not just cheap long shots) is improbable too. Plus pre-event timing (anchored to when the market repriced, not when it resolved) and the repeat-offender pattern of being early-and-right on several separate surprises.
Why one view
A flagged trade and a flagged wallet were the same unit (one row per account), so two tabs were redundant. Merging them gives one ranked list, one bar of evidence, and no double-counting.
